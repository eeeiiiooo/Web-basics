Limited migrant rights pose significant dangers both to individuals and to broader society. When migrants are denied legal protections, access to healthcare, fair wages, and safe working conditions, they become vulnerable to exploitation, abuse, and human trafficking. This marginalization often forces them into informal or illegal labor markets, where their lack of legal status can be used against them. Without the ability to report crimes or seek justice, migrants may endure dangerous environments in silence. Furthermore, limiting rights fosters social exclusion and resentment, undermining social cohesion and economic stability. Ultimately, denying migrants their rights erodes human dignity and weakens the foundational principles of justice and equality in any society.
